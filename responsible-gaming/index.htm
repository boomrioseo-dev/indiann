Responsible Gambling Rules and Tips

Gambling should remain a fun experience, and not become a source of stress. Parimatch encourages following the recommendations that will help control gambling behavior:

  • Set limits: before you start, set realistic limits for yourself. Determine the maximum amount you are willing to deposit/bet/lose while playing. This will help you avoid making impulsive decisions;
  • Take breaks: It is important to take breaks from time to time while gambling. Continuous gambling can lead to fatigue and poor decision-making. Taking a break from gambling allows you to assess your emotional (and financial) situation;
  • Don’t gamble when you are stressed: When you are stressed, anxious, or upset, your thinking becomes less clear and you are more likely to make rash decisions. Gambling should not be a way to escape the negative aspects of life;
  • View gambling as entertainment: Treat gambling as a form of entertainment, not as a way to earn money, much less a regular one. The odds are often not in the player’s favor;
  • Know your limits: Always be aware of how much you can afford to lose, and do not gamble beyond your financial means, especially if you are in debt. It is important to know when to stop.

Signs of Gambling Addiction

Gambling addiction can have extremely destructive consequences in your life. It is important to recognize the signs of gambling addiction early on. Here are some signs that may indicate that gambling is slowly becoming a real problem:

  • Spending more time gambling than you initially intended: If you are consistently spending more time gambling than you initially planned, this may mean that you are losing control;
  • Unsuccessful attempts to stop gambling: If you have tried to stop gambling but have failed, this may already be a sign of an addiction;
  • Borrowing money or selling personal items to have money to gamble: If you borrow or even sell your things to have money to gamble, then we are talking about a clear addiction;
  • Gambling beyond your financial means: You spend money that you cannot afford to lose. This often leads to serious consequences, both financial and emotional, sooner or later;
  • Lying about the time or money you spend on gambling: If you hide this from your family or friends, this is a clear sign that gambling has become a negative part of your life;
  • Feeling hopeless or depressed about gambling: If you feel depressed or hopeless about gambling (especially because of your losses), it is important to seek professional support without delay.

If you notice any of these in yourself (or your loved ones), you should seek appropriate support immediately.
